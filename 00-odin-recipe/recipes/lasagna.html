Lasagna

What is a Lasagna?

is an Italian dish made with layers of pasta, meat sauce, and cheese, baked until bubbly and golden brown.

It's a hearty and comforting meal often enjoyed with family and friends.

Ingredients

  • Lasagna sheets
  • Ground beef or Italian sausage
  • Tomato sauce
  • Ricotta cheese
  • Mozzarella cheese
  • Parmesan cheese
  • Eggs
  • Garlic
  • Onion
  • Spices (oregano, basil, thyme, salt, pepper)

Steps:

  1. Prepare the meat sauce: Brown ground beef or Italian sausage in a large skillet. Add chopped onion and garlic, cook until softened. Pour in tomato sauce, and season with oregano, basil, thyme, salt, and pepper. Simmer for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Make the ricotta mixture: In a bowl, combine ricotta cheese, beaten eggs, grated Parmesan cheese, and a small amount of meat sauce. Season with salt and pepper.
  3. Assemble the lasagna: Spread a thin layer of meat sauce in the bottom of a baking dish. Layer lasagna sheets, ricotta mixture, and meat sauce, repeating until all ingredients are used, ending with a layer of meat sauce and mozzarella cheese.
  4. Bake: Cover with foil and bake at 375°F (190°C) for 30 minutes. Uncover and bake for an additional 15-20 minutes, or until bubbly and golden brown. Let cool for a few minutes before serving.
